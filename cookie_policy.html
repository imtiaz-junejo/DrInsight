What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, tablet, or smartphone) when you visit a website. They allow the website to remember information about your visit — such as your preferred language, font size settings, or whether you've already accepted our cookie notice.

💾

🔄

→

✅ What Cookies CANNOT Do
  • ❌ Not viruses or malware
  • ❌ Cannot access other files on your device
  • ❌ Cannot carry personal medical records
  • ❌ Cannot execute programs
  • ❌ Cannot identify you personally without your consent

Similar technologies also covered: Web beacons (tiny tracking images) · Pixel tags (ad conversion tracking) · Local storage · Session storage · We do NOT use browser fingerprinting.

Why We Use Cookies
✅ We Use Cookies To
  • Keep the platform functioning correctly
  • Remember your preferences (font size, contrast mode, language)
  • Keep you logged into your account across pages
  • Understand how readers navigate and use our content

🚫 We Do NOT Use Cookies To
  • Collect sensitive health or medical information
  • Build profiles of your medical conditions or treatments
  • Sell your data to third parties
  • Track your activity across unrelated websites without disclosure
  • Target you with health-related advertising based on medical behaviour

How Long Cookies Last
⏱️

Session Cookies

Temporary — exist only while your browser is open. Deleted automatically when you close your browser. Used for: login sessions, CSRF tokens, temporary navigation state.

📅

Persistent Cookies

Remain on your device for a set period. Duration: 24 hours to 2 years. Used for: remembering preferences, analytics measurement, ad tracking. See Section 4 for full reference.

Cookie durations may be shorter if you: clear browser cookies manually · use private/incognito browsing · use a cookie-blocking extension · set your browser to reject cookies.

How to Manage Your Cookie Preferences

Click the button below (or the floating 🍪 button at bottom-left) to open our interactive Cookie Preference Panel and control each category individually.

Browser-Level Cookie Controls

🌐 Google Chrome

▾
  1. Click the three-dot menu → Settings
  2. Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  3. Choose your preferred cookie setting
  4. To delete existing cookies: Clear browsing data → Cookies and other site data

🦊 Mozilla Firefox

▾
  1. Click the hamburger menu → Settings
  2. Privacy & Security → Enhanced Tracking Protection
  3. Select Standard, Strict, or Custom
  4. To clear cookies: Clear Data → Cookies and Site Data

🧭 Safari (Mac)

▾
  1. Safari menu → Preferences → Privacy
  2. Manage Website Data to view/delete cookies
  3. Check "Prevent cross-site tracking"

📱 Safari (iPhone / iPad)

▾
  1. Settings → Safari → Privacy & Security
  2. Toggle "Prevent Cross-Site Tracking"
  3. "Block All Cookies" option available if needed

🔵 Microsoft Edge

▾
  1. Three-dot menu → Settings → Cookies and site permissions
  2. Manage and delete cookies and site data
  3. Choose Block, Allow, or Session only

⚙️ Opera

▾
  1. Opera menu → Settings → Advanced → Privacy & security
  2. Site Settings → Cookies and site data
  3. Choose your preferred level of cookie control
⚠️ Note

Disabling cookies at the browser level may affect the functionality of other websites you visit, not just ours. We recommend using our Cookie Preference Center for platform-specific control.

10
Do Not Track (DNT) Signals

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal that can be sent to websites. Currently there is no universal standard for how websites must respond to DNT signals.

Our current DNT response: We acknowledge DNT signals. We do not currently alter our cookie behaviour in response to DNT signals due to lack of standardisation. We recommend using our Cookie Preference Center for direct control over tracking. We support the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal where technically feasible, and will update this section if a universal DNT standard is established.

Cookie Policy for Mobile Apps

If you use the MedAuthority mobile application, this section applies. Mobile apps may use device identifiers (IDFA on iOS, GAID on Android) instead of traditional cookies, app analytics tools (Firebase Analytics), and push notification tokens (only with explicit permission).

📱 How to Control Mobile Tracking
  • iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → Toggle off per app
  • Android: Settings → Privacy → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalisation
