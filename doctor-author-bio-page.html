👤 About Dr. James Okafor

Dr. James Okafor is a board-certified Consultant Neurologist at NYU Langone Medical Center with 12 years of clinical experience specialising in headache medicine, neuro-oncology, and movement disorders. He has authored over 64 peer-reviewed articles and is a recognised expert in migraine pathophysiology and emerging CGRP therapies.

Dr. Okafor's passion for neurology was ignited during his undergraduate studies when he witnessed the transformative impact of early neurological intervention on stroke outcomes. He graduated top of his class from the University of Lagos College of Medicine before completing his MD and MRCP at King's College London, followed by a prestigious fellowship in Headache Medicine at Mayo Clinic.

His clinical philosophy centres on a patient-first, evidence-led approach — he believes that complex neurological conditions are best managed through the intersection of rigorous science, compassionate communication, and shared decision-making with patients and their families.

Dr. Okafor leads active research programs investigating CGRP monoclonal antibodies in chronic migraine, cortical spreading depression biomarkers, and AI-assisted neurological diagnosis. He has been awarded over $2.4M in research grants and has presented at the American Academy of Neurology, European Headache Federation, and World Congress of Neurology.

Beyond clinical practice, he is deeply committed to neurological health education in underserved communities and has led free neurology clinics across West Africa. He serves as an assistant professor at NYU Grossman School of Medicine and mentors over 30 neurology residents annually.
